HOW TO PLAY TOP LANE

Your guide on how to play top lane

In League of Legends, the top lane is one of the three lanes on Summoner's Rift, and is usually played by champions who have strong sustain and durability. Top lane is a solo lane, meaning you will be primarily responsible for controlling your own lane, and you will have less interaction with your team in the early game compared to other roles like Jungle or Support.

CHOOSE A CHAMPION

Mastering top lane champs

There are a variety of champions that can be played in top lane, but the most common are bruisers, tanks, and some ranged champions. Some popular top lane champions include Garen, Renekton, and Darius. If you're new to the game, it's best to choose one champion and focus on mastering their abilities and playstyle. This will help you build a solid foundation and improve your overall performance in the top lane

LANE TRADING

Understanding the Art of Damage Exchange

Auto-attacks in League of Legends are key for lane trading, helping to chip away at opponents' health and prompting defensive play.

In lane trading, using spells effectively means considering factors like cooldowns, costs, damage potential, opponent's position, and health to gain an edge and secure kills.

Knowing your own and your opponent's ability cooldowns is key to deciding when to trade lanes or initiate fights.

WARDING

Mastering the Map through Sight

Warding in League of Legends is the act of placing vision-providing items known as wards on the map. These wards reveal the surrounding area and provide crucial information about enemy movements.

WAVE MANAGEMENT

The Art of Wave Management in League of Legends

Wave management in League of Legends involves strategic control of minion waves to gain advantages, such as freezing to deny farm, pushing for map pressure, and slow pushing to set up dives or sieges. It's a vital skill for laning and overall game control.

GROUPING OR SPLITPUSHING?

Will you group with your team, or splitpush on your own?

In League of Legends, teams use either "Grouping" to focus on objectives and team fights together or "Splitpushing" to pressure different lanes separately, requiring the enemy team to make challenging decisions.

Grouping is when champions come together and move as a team to achieve common objectives

OR

Splitting is when a champion separates from their team to apply pressure in a different lane.

SUPPORT YOUR JUNGLER

Help your jungler get ahead and secure objectives

In League of Legends, top laners support their junglers by warding key areas, controlling their lane to help in fights or objective control, communicating crucial information, and using teleport to aid in pivotal battles around major objectives.
